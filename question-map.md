# Living question map
### ODIN Isle of Wight Pilot

Every significant question that emerges from every session is recorded here,
verbatim, with its source and disciplinary annotations.

This document does not curate. It does not edit. It does not improve questions
into more sensible versions. It records them exactly as they were asked, by whom,
and when.

The questions are primary sources. The annotations are secondary.
The map grows with every session.

---

## How to read this map

Each question is a node. The disciplines listed beneath it show which fields
of knowledge the question touches — not as a curriculum exercise, but as evidence
that self-organised curiosity naturally crosses every boundary that formal
education erects.

This document is also a challenge to the siloed structure of the school curriculum.
A 9-year-old asking "can water get sick?" is simultaneously doing environmental
law, microbiology, philosophy of rights, and political economy. The map makes
that visible.

## Seed questions (pre-session, from project design phase)

These are questions that emerged during the design of the pilot itself —
not from children yet, but from the process of thinking about what children
might ask.

### Can water get sick?
- **Source**: Project design phase, March 2025
- **Disciplines**: Environmental law, microbiology, philosophy of rights,
  political economy, public health
- **Notes**: Anticipated as an emergent question from the 5–7 age group SOLE
  session. Included here as a benchmark — if this question emerges naturally
  from a session, it confirms the SOLE method is working.

### Who owns the water?
- **Source**: Project design phase, March 2025
- **Disciplines**: Property law, commons theory, political philosophy,
  environmental economics, indigenous rights, UK water privatisation history
- **Notes**: Designed as the opening question for the 14–16 secondary SOLE
  session. Its appearance here is a design choice; its reappearance in a session
  will be an empirical finding.

### Could we live without water?
- **Source**: Project design phase, March 2025
- **Disciplines**: Biology, survival science, climate science, geopolitics,
  food systems, philosophy of necessity
- **Notes**: Opening question for the 7–11 primary SOLE session.
